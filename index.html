Biography

Hi, It’s Jia Yibo (Terentia)! I hold a Master’s degree in ASEANStudies from the University of Malaya(QS ranking 60), where I graduated with distinction (GPA 3.89/4). My studies, supervised by Professor Fumitaka Furuoka, focused on Southeast Asian regionalism, political economy, social customs and immigrant groups, and security. I critically examined China’s foreign aid policies and their moral implications, while also exploring the social impact of Chinese diasporas in Southeast Asia.

Before pursuing my graduate studies, I had the privilege of working with the Shanghai Cooperation Organization, where I collaborated with high-level diplomats to enhance economic cooperation among member states. This experience sparked my passion for international relations and strengthened my commitment to cross-cultural dialogue.

I’m also deeply committed to public service and empowerment. I volunteered in rural mountain areas, where I taught and mentored students from underprivileged backgrounds. Since 2020, I have been providing free academic counseling to female students, I believe in the power of education to create opportunities and drive change. As AI and robotic tech embed in daily life, I am curious about how they might reshape care, gender roles, and emotional labor. I hope I can pursue this research in the future and share the results with the world’s most vulnerable.

Research Interests

Curiosity is the driving force behind my research, and the opportunity to collaborate with exceptional people is what fuels it even further! I am particularly curious about the following topics:

Human–Robot Interaction: study how humans relate to robots emotionally and socially; for example, how people trust, care for, or resist domestic robots.

Feminist Technology Studies: examine how gender influences technology design and use; like questioning male-dominated tech narratives and promoting inclusive innovation.

Moral Realism in Foreign Aid: analyze the ethical foundations of international aid; for instance, assessing whether foreign aid aligns with moral duties, not just political interests.

Domestic Robotics and Care: explore the role of robots in caregiving and household tasks; such as supporting elderly care or reducing emotional labor for women.

Cross-cultural Technology Use: investigate how different cultures adapt and interact with the same technologies; for example, how AI assistants are used differently in Asia vs. Europe.

ASEAN Regionalism and Social Impact: study how ASEAN cooperation affects society; like improving education access, promoting equality, or shaping regional identity.

Migration and Human Security: focus on how migration affects personal safety and rights; including refugee protection, integration policies, and climate-driven displacement.

Non-human-centered research: benefit nature and wildlife, like social animal behavior and protecting natural habitats.
