Biography

I hold a Master’s degree in ASEAN Studies from the University of Malaya (QS ranking 60), where I graduated with distinction (GPA 3.89/4). My studies, supervised by Professor Fumitaka Furuoka, focused on Southeast Asian regionalism, political economy, and security.

My academic journey is deeply shaped by my cross-cultural and interdisciplinary experiences. I come from China and have lived and studied in Malaysia, while also conducting research on China’s foreign aid in Cambodia. My master’s thesis explored the moral dimensions of China’s Belt and Road Initiative projects through qualitative interviews.

I’ve worked in international education organizations, managing programs between Chinese and foreign institutions. As AI and robotic tech embed in daily life, I became curious about how they reshape care, gender roles, and emotional labor.

Research Interests

Research Experience

STAGC: Attribute Graph Clustering Driven Cell Map of Single Cell Spatial Transcriptome
First author, under reviewing

STAGC is a graph-based self-encoder clustering algorithm for spatial transcriptomics. It refines clustering via self-training and soft labels from graph embeddings. It also uses expression similarity instead of just spatial distance.
